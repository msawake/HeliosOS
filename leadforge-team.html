Identify Skill Clusters
▶

Group all human tasks by the expertise they require. Each cluster is a potential hire.

Cluster A — Legal & Compliance

  • Contract review, signing, execution
  • GDPR/CAN-SPAM compliance oversight
  • DPA review
  • Outreach template compliance (4h SLA!)
  • Risk assessment on escalated items
  • Regulatory filings

Cluster B — Finance & Accounting

  • Financial transactions $1K-$5K
  • Client invoicing oversight
  • Ad spend approval >$500 (12h SLA)
  • Tax filing oversight
  • Budget allocation

  • Approve commitments $5K-$10K

  • Daily audit sampling (15 emails + sequences)

Cluster E — Client Relationship

  • QBR scheduling & participation

  • Churn risk escalation handling

Map Clusters to People (Minimize Headcount)
▶

At startup scale, we combine clusters where skills overlap:

Person 1: Founder/CEO
Cluster C (strategy) + Cluster E (clients) = 13-22h/week
Strategic decisions + client QBRs + final escalation authority. Same skillset: executive judgment + client-facing.
Person 2: Fractional Legal Counsel
Cluster A = 15-20h/week
Legal expertise is non-negotiable — cannot merge with other roles. But 15-20h/week doesn't justify full-time at 5 clients.
Person 3: Finance & Ops Manager
Cluster B (finance) + Cluster D (audit) = 22-33h/week
Financial approvals + daily quality audits. Same workflow: review AI output, approve/reject from dashboard. Full-time role from day one.

Result: 2.5 people at launch (1 full-time founder + 1 fractional counsel + 1 full-time ops manager).

6
Scale Economics: Why Margins Improve
▶

AI agents scale linearly (add tokens). Humans scale sub-linearly:

Contract templates reused
Legal work per new client drops 50% after first 5 clients (standard MSA, DPA templates)
Audit sampling stays %
10% of 150 emails = 15. 10% of 450 emails = 45. One person can still handle 45 reviews/day.
Financial approvals batch
One CFO can approve $5K for 5 clients or 30 clients in the same decision window.
Token costs are linear
10M tokens/day at $0.015/1K = ~$150/day = ~$4.5K/month. Even at 3x, still <$15K/month.

Critical Bottleneck Analysis

Where the system breaks if a human is unavailable.

Highest Risk
Legal Counsel Unavailability
If fractional counsel is sick or unavailable for 48h, ALL contract approvals auto-deny (per default_denials config). New client onboarding stops. Outreach compliance reviews (4h SLA) expire.
Backup counsel on retainer ($2-3K/month standby). Pre-approved contract templates for standard MSA terms. Compliance review delegation to Ops Manager for routine template approvals.
Highest Volume
Finance/Ops Manager Overload
This person handles 8-12 approvals + 15-20 audit items daily. The 4h content SLA means checking the dashboard before noon. If sick, content and financial approvals queue up within hours.
Mobile-friendly HITL dashboard with push notifications. CEO as backup approver for financial <$1K. Content SLA extended to 8h as contingency. Hire compliance analyst earlier if volume spikes.
Single Point of Failure
Founder for >$5K Decisions
The exec-cfo agent can approve up to $5K, but $5K-$10K requires CEO (= founder). If founder is on vacation, these decisions stall. Auto-deny at 24h could kill a deal.
Formal delegation authority: when founder is OOO, Ops Manager gets temporary $10K approval limit. Document in company-config.yaml. Set up mobile approval flow.
Sampling Bias
10% Audit Coverage Gap
Only 10% of outreach emails get human review. The other 90% rely entirely on AI compliance checking. A systematic error in agent behavior could go undetected for days.
Adaptive sampling: increase to 30% for first week of new campaigns or new clients. Add automated compliance scoring that flags anomalies for 100% human review. Weekly trend analysis.
