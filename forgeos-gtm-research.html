Play #1: Insurance Claims Payment Automation

Industry: Insurance (44) Function: AP/AR (43) Combined: 87/100 Deal: $3-5K/mo

The pitch: "Your claims team processes FNOL manually, matches payments to reserves in spreadsheets, and chases subrogation in email. We deploy AI that ingests FNOL from any channel, extracts structured data, matches against policy terms, sets initial reserves, and routes to the right adjuster — in minutes, not days."

Why it wins

  • Claims resolution drops from 30 days to 7.5 days avg
  • 90% of carriers tested AI but only 22% reached production — they want this but can't build it
  • State DOI regulations block generic ChatGPT from this domain
  • CFO signs off — 1% combined ratio improvement = millions

Expansion path

  • FNOL intake → Claims payment matching → Subrogation tracking → Renewal underwriting data assembly → Policy endorsement processing
  • One client = 3-5 workflows = $12-20K/mo within 6 months
  • Each workflow makes the next easier (shared data, trust built)

Target: Mid-market carriers and MGAs, 50-500 employees. Buyer: CFO or VP Claims. Cycle: 6-10 weeks.

  • Prior auth market growing 10x YoY

  • Target: physician groups 20-200 providers

Deal: $3-5K/mo — Cycle: 8-12 weeks (HIPAA adds time)

Sales Cycle Gates

Week 1-2

Champion Buy-In

Internal person who owns the pain must believe you can solve it. Without a champion, nothing moves. Discovery call + demo on their data.

Week 2-4

Technical Validation

Can you integrate with their tools? IT sends security questionnaire (50-200 questions). SOC 2 Type II is table stakes. Pre-complete a SIG Lite or CAIQ to cut 2-4 weeks.

Week 3-6

Pilot / POC

Mid-market buyers almost always want a pilot. 2-week free pilot, minimal client effort (3-4 hours total), clear success metrics agreed upfront. "We run the pilot. You give us access and a point of contact."

Week 5-8

Business Case + Contract

Your champion presents internally with an ROI calculator you provide. Legal redlines MSA (2-3 rounds). Key sticking points: liability, data ownership, SLA penalties, termination for convenience.

Week 6-10

Procurement + Signature

Budget confirmation, vendor onboarding (W-9, insurance), payment terms (Net-30 standard). Finance confirms. Signature.

Top 8 Objections & Counters

"We're worried about data security and privacy."
Counter: Lead with your security posture on the first call. SOC 2 Type II, encryption at rest/in transit, BAA for healthcare, no model training on client data. Hand them a pre-completed SIG Lite before they ask. Make security your opening move, not a response to their concern.
"We tried AI/automation before and it didn't work."
Counter: "RPA broke because bots are brittle — one screen change kills them. Our AI understands context and adapts. More importantly, we manage it — you don't need internal expertise. Here's a client who had the same experience and saw [specific metric]."
"$3K/month is expensive for one process."
Counter: Reframe from cost to ROI. "You currently spend $8K/month on this process. At $3K/month, you save $5K/month — 167% ROI from day one. The question isn't whether $3K is expensive, it's whether saving $60K/year is worth it." Always quantify their current cost first.
"We can build this ourselves with ChatGPT."
Counter: "You could. The question is: hire 2 AI engineers ($300K/yr), spend 6-12 months building, then maintain it forever? Or get production-quality automation in 30 days for $36K/year? Our clients tried DIY first — they came to us after burning 6 months."
"What if the AI makes mistakes? Who's liable?"
Counter: "Every process has human-in-the-loop quality gates. AI handles 80% autonomously; outputs above your threshold get human review. Our SLA guarantees X% accuracy. Fun fact: AI with human review is actually more accurate than humans alone — it catches things humans miss."
"Our processes are too unique for automation."
Counter: "Every client says that, and they're partially right. That's why we're a managed service, not software. We map your actual process (not the documented one) and build around your reality. Here are three clients with similarly 'unique' processes."
"We need to see 6 months of results before committing."
Counter: "We offer month-to-month after a 90-day initial term. No long-term lock-in. If we're not delivering value, walk away with 30 days' notice. We keep clients because of results, not contracts." (This kills the enterprise vendor comparison.)
"What happens to our employees?"
Counter: Frame as augmentation. "Your AP team spends 30 hrs/week on data entry. We automate the data entry so they focus on exception handling and vendor negotiations. Most clients redeploy staff, they don't reduce headcount. Your best people finally get to do the work they were hired for."

Dealbreakers to Avoid

These will kill deals regardless of how good your product is. Address them before they're raised.

Must-Haves (Day 1)

  • SOC 2 Type II — table stakes for any regulated buyer. Start the audit now.
  • Human-in-the-loop — no mid-market buyer in 2026 trusts fully autonomous AI on critical processes. Always offer review checkpoints.
  • Month-to-month flexibility — no 12+ month lock-in. 90-day initial term, then month-to-month. Buyers have been burned.
  • Core system integrations — Salesforce, NetSuite, QuickBooks, Workday, Zendesk, Guidewire. If you can't connect, the deal dies.

Must-Haves (Month 3)

  • 2-3 referenceable clients — mid-market buyers always ask for references. Lukewarm references kill deals.
  • Explainable AI — buyers are AI-literate. "It's a black box" is a dealbreaker. Explain what models you use and how decisions are made.
  • US-timezone support — offshore-only support signals low quality. Managed service = managed by people they can reach.
  • BAA for healthcare — non-negotiable for any healthcare admin play. Pre-sign it.

Strategic Insights

The "Managed" Part Is Your Moat

In Tier 1 industries (insurance, healthcare, financial services), buyers explicitly distrust fully automated AI. A doctor won't trust a chatbot to handle prior auth appeals. A carrier won't let unsupervised AI adjudicate claims. Your 800-person agency — human expertise deploying and managing AI — is a feature, not overhead. In Tier 3 industries (retail, travel), the managed model is over-engineered and overpriced. Stay in Tier 1.

Regulation = Moat = Pricing Power

The three highest-scoring industries (Insurance 44, Healthcare 44, Financial Services 42) are also the three most regulated. This is not coincidence. Regulation blocks ChatGPT, Gemini, and generic SaaS tools from competing. A $30/seat Copilot subscription cannot navigate state DOI claims handling regulations. Your domain-specific managed service can. This is why you can charge $3-5K/month where others charge $30/seat.

Data Entry Is the Trojan Horse

Document processing scored 43/50 as a function — tied for #1 with AP/AR. It has the fastest time-to-value (1-2 weeks), clearest per-unit ROI, and lowest buyer risk. Use it as the foot in the door. Once you're processing their invoices, the upsell to AP automation, then claims matching, then compliance reporting becomes a natural expansion conversation with the same buyer.

Your Agency's Client Base Is the Distribution

CAC for new logos via cold outreach: $3-5K. CAC for expansion within existing agency clients: $0-500. Your 800-person agency's existing relationships are worth more than any marketing campaign. Every account manager should be trained to identify workflow automation opportunities. A formalized cross-sell program with internal incentives could generate 50%+ of year-one pipeline.

Final Verdict: Where to Attack

The Optimal Sequence

  1. Month 1-3: Insurance carriers + AP/Claims automation. Highest combined score (87/100). CFO signs alone. Prove the model here.
  2. Month 2-4: Staffing agencies + Candidate sourcing. Fastest sales cycle (4-6 weeks). Fully digital data. Quick wins build team confidence.
  3. Month 4-6: Healthcare admin + Prior auth/Doc processing. Highest pain (14 hrs/physician/week). Requires BAA + SOC 2 — start prep in Month 1.
  4. Month 6-9: Financial services + KYC/Compliance. Highest pricing power ($4-5K/mo). Stickiest clients (<5% churn). Longest cycle — start conversations in Month 3.
  5. Month 9-12: Expand into Tier 2 (Logistics, Professional Services) based on which verticals your team has developed domain expertise in.

What NOT to do: Don't spread across all 12 industries. Don't chase retail or non-profit. Don't compete with Gemini on per-seat pricing. Don't sell software — sell outcomes. The Palantir model wins when you go deep in 3-4 verticals, not wide across 12.
