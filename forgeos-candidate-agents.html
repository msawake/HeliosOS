10 Candidate Agents for Enterprise Deployment

Extending the 4-agent roadmap with 10 additional autonomous agents — scored, phased, and mapped to ForgeOS capabilities

Context: These 10 agents complement the original 4 (Client Data Analyzer, Invoice Processor, Email Responder, Activity Manager). Same scoring system: 8 dimensions, 1-5 each, 40 max. Same design constraints: autonomous, always-on, event-driven, or scheduled — no chat-based agents. Same graduated autonomy model: Shadow → Supervised → HITL-Gated → Autonomous.

Timesheet Anomaly Detector
Scheduled Haiku Phase 1

What It Does

Runs weekly (Friday PM). Pulls timesheets from the time-tracking system, compares hours logged against project budgets and allocations. Flags: over-billing on capped contracts, under-reporting by team members, entries without project codes, hours exceeding physical limits, and patterns suggesting time-padding.

Output: Anomaly report to project managers with flagged entries and reasons. No modifications to timesheets — purely analytical.

ForgeOS Configuration

  • Schedule: "0 17 * * 5" (Friday 5 PM)
  • Model: Haiku — structured numerical comparison, no complex reasoning needed
  • MCP: Time-tracking API (read), Google Sheets (budget data)
  • Reuses: invoice-categorizer pattern (structured data comparison + anomaly flagging)
  • HITL: None. Report is informational. Managers decide action.

Risks & Mitigations

  • False positives embarrass employees: Reports go ONLY to managers, never team-wide. First 4 weeks: managers validate before any follow-up.
  • Threshold miscalibration: Start conservative (flag only >20% deviation), tighten over time based on manager feedback.

SLA Watchdog
Always-On Haiku Phase 1

What It Does

Continuously monitors all client SLAs across systems (Jira ticket response times, delivery deadlines, uptime commitments). Predicts breaches 24 hours before they happen based on current velocity and remaining work. Sends graduated alerts: warning at 24h, escalation at 12h, critical at 4h.

Output: Slack alerts to account managers and team leads. Escalation to department heads if breach is imminent. Read-only — never modifies tickets or deadlines.

  • Execution: Always-on, 5-minute tick cycle
  • Model: Haiku — reads structured SLA data, compares deadlines, simple arithmetic
  • MCP: Jira (read tickets + SLAs), Google Calendar (delivery dates), CRM (client SLA terms)

  • Prediction inaccuracy: Use conservative estimates (assume no work happens on weekends/after-hours). Better to over-alert than miss a breach.

Contract Renewal Tracker
Scheduled Event Sonnet Phase 1

What It Does

Scans all active contracts (vendor and client) daily. Extracts renewal dates, auto-renewal clauses, cancellation windows, and price escalation terms. Sends graduated alerts at 90, 60, and 30 days before expiry. At 60 days, generates a renewal brief: contract summary, historical spend, market alternatives, and recommended negotiation points.

Output: Renewal timeline dashboard + proactive briefs to account/procurement managers. Event-driven trigger when a contract document is uploaded to scan it immediately.

Meeting Action Extractor
Event-Driven Sonnet Phase 2

What It Does

Triggers when a meeting recording or transcript becomes available (event: meeting.ended). Processes the transcript to extract: key decisions made, action items with assignees and deadlines, open questions, and a 3-paragraph meeting summary. Creates Jira tickets for each action item and sends a digest to all attendees.

Output: Structured meeting summary + auto-created Jira tasks. Writes to Jira and email.

  • Trigger: meeting.ended event (from calendar integration or recording platform webhook)
  • Model: Sonnet — needs strong comprehension of conversational context to identify commitments vs. casual mentions
  • MCP: Google Calendar (attendee list), Jira (create tasks), Google Workspace (send digest email)

  • Confidential meetings processed inappropriately: Allow organizer to tag meetings as "no-AI" in calendar. Agent skips those.

Employee Access Auditor
Scheduled Sonnet Phase 2

What It Does

Weekly audit of system access across all tools (Google Workspace, Jira, CRM, SAP, Slack, code repos). Flags: dormant accounts (no login in 60+ days), orphaned permissions (departed employees still active), over-privileged users (admin rights not justified by role), and shared service accounts with no owner. Produces an access risk report for IT/Security.

Output: Access audit report with remediation recommendations. Can optionally create tickets in Jira for IT to action. Does NOT revoke access directly (Phase 2 caution).

Expense Report Processor
Event-Driven Sonnet Phase 2

What It Does

Triggers on expense.submitted event. Validates each line item against company expense policy: per-diem limits, allowed categories, required receipts, client-billable vs. internal classification. Flags policy violations, duplicate submissions, and suspicious patterns (round numbers, weekend meals for non-travel). Routes compliant reports for manager approval. Syncs approved expenses to the accounting system.

Output: Validated expense report with annotations + policy violation flags. Writes to accounting system after approval chain completes.

Vendor Payment Reconciler
Scheduled Opus Phase 2

What It Does

Runs daily. Performs three-way matching: vendor invoices vs. purchase orders vs. delivery/service receipts. Identifies: unmatched invoices (no PO), partial deliveries, price discrepancies, duplicate vendor invoices, and invoices approaching payment terms (Net-30, Net-60). Prepares a payment batch recommendation with priority ordering (early-payment discounts first, approaching-deadline second).

Output: Reconciliation report + payment batch recommendation for AP team. Optionally creates payment records in SAP after HITL approval.

Compliance & Policy Monitor
Always-On Opus Phase 3

What It Does

Continuously monitors regulatory sources (government gazettes, industry bodies, legal databases) for changes affecting the company's operations. Compares new regulations against internal policies, contracts, and processes. When a gap is detected, generates an impact assessment: what changed, who's affected, what needs updating, and by when. Tracks remediation progress.

Output: Regulatory change alerts + impact assessments + compliance gap reports. Can create Jira tickets for remediation tracking.

  • MCP: Web scraping/feeds (regulatory sources), Google Workspace (internal policies), Jira (remediation tracking)

  • Misinterpretation of legal language: All assessments are marked DRAFT. Compliance officer validates before the company acts.

Client Onboarding Pipeline
Event-Driven Opus Phase 3

What It Does

Triggers on client.signed event. Orchestrates the entire onboarding workflow as a DAG: CRM record creation → welcome email sequence → project workspace setup (Jira board, shared drive, Slack channel) → kickoff meeting scheduling → document collection (NDA, SOW, contact list) → access provisioning → billing setup in Stripe. Tracks checklist completion and escalates stalled items.

Output: Fully onboarded client with all systems provisioned. Multi-system writes across 5+ platforms. Client-facing communications (welcome emails).

  • MCP: CRM, Google Workspace (email, Drive, Calendar), Jira, Slack, Stripe

Risks & Mitigations

  • Botched first impression: Welcome email and kickoff scheduling are the client-facing touchpoints. Both go through HITL until the template library is proven (>20 successful onboardings).

Proposal / SOW Generator
Event-Driven Opus Phase 3

What It Does

Triggers when an opportunity reaches a configurable stage in the CRM (e.g., "Proposal Requested"). Orchestrates: competitive intelligence gathering on the prospect, historical pricing analysis for similar deals, scope definition based on discovery notes, SOW draft using company templates with client-specific customization, pricing calculation with margin guardrails, and legal clause selection based on deal size and risk profile. Produces a ready-to-review proposal document.

Output: Complete proposal/SOW document in Google Docs. May be shared with the client — highest-stakes written deliverable.

ForgeOS Configuration

  • Trigger: opportunity.stage_changed event from CRM (stage = "Proposal Requested")
  • Model: Opus — must synthesize competitive intel, pricing logic, legal clauses, and persuasive writing into a cohesive document
  • MCP: CRM (opportunity data, contact info), Google Workspace (Docs for output, Sheets for pricing models), Web (competitor research)
  • Reuses: proposal-orchestrator practical agent (exact use case: research → pricing → writing → legal → assembly) + contract-checker (legal clause selection) + competitor-monitor (competitive positioning)
  • Workflow: 5-step DAG via TaskGraphBuilder: research → pricing → drafting → legal review → assembly
  • HITL: Always. Every proposal is reviewed by the account executive before client delivery. Pricing reviewed by finance if >$50K deal. Legal clauses reviewed if custom terms needed.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Wrong pricing (under-bid = margin loss, over-bid = lost deal): Pricing guardrails in KnowledgeBase: minimum margin by service type, maximum discount without VP approval. Agent cannot generate pricing outside guardrails.
  • Generic-sounding proposal ("AI slop"): Use Opus, not Sonnet/Haiku. Require client-specific details in every section. Reject drafts that don't reference at least 3 specifics from discovery notes.
  • Outdated competitive intel: Competitive data refreshed by competitor-monitor agent weekly. Proposals cite data freshness date.
  • Sending wrong proposal to wrong client: Proposal document title includes client name. HITL reviewer verifies before sharing.

The compounding effect: 14 agents connected by events create an autonomous operations layer. The SLA Watchdog catches risks the Activity Manager acts on. The Invoice Processor feeds the Reconciler which feeds payment batches. The Compliance Monitor flags regulatory changes that the Contract Tracker applies to upcoming renewals. No single agent works in isolation — the system is greater than the sum of its parts.
